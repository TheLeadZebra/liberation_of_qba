### Baldur's Bones
(2+ players)
At the beginning of a game of Baldur's Bones, each player would put an agreed-upon ante into a pot. Each player needed three six-sided dice in order to play. After everyone paid their wagers, players would roll their starting dice and then play would proceed clockwise, with the host of the game intended to always go last.

On a given turn, a player could choose to roll or "stand", meaning they passed their turn over to the next player. If a player chose to "roll", they were given an additional die to roll. Once the roll was made, the player would once again be presented with the two options of "stand" or "roll" until they made the decision to end their turn.

The objective of the game was to have the highest numerical total on one's collective dice, with the highest numerical value allowed being 21. If a "roll" resulted in a collective value higher than 21, the player was considered to have "bust" and be out of the game. The player with the highest total at the end of play would win the ante pot.


### Coin Slots
(Solo Play)
These slot machines are simple clockwork devices that accept coins. Some refer to playing a slot machine as "Tugging [[The World/Deities#Astilabor\|Astilabor]]'s Arm".
Each machine has a spring-loaded lever and five identical spinning cylinders called reels.
Six golden, themed runes are painted on each reel.

A player may insert from 1 to 9 coins of the same type into the machine's slot and pulls the level, which causes the reels to spin, stop, and display a row of five runes.
The player wins by matching three or more runes.

To determine the result of a pull, have the player roll 5d6 (the dice represent the five reels of runes). 
The players goal is to roll as many of the same number on the dice as possible.

The payout, if any, varies according to the results, as shown in the Coin Slots Payout table below.

##### Coin Slots Payout

| 5d6 Result      | Payout                       |
| --------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Three of a Kind | 2-to-1 (2x + original bet)   |
| Four of a Kind  | 4-to-1 (4x + original bet)   |
| Five of a Kind  | 10-to-1 (10x + original bet) |
### Dice Nice Dice (DnD)
(2+ Players)
Each player places a bet, bets must be matched by all participants.

Each player then rolls a d20.

The participant with the highest roll wins, unless:
- Any participant has rolled a 13, they automatically lose
- Any participant has rolled a 7, they automatically win

The pot is split evenly among any joint winners.

### Garyx's Gamble
(2+ Players)
Each player places an ante into the pot. The ante can be any amount, but must be matched by all players.

Each player places a hand into a magical mechanism, which locks their wrist in place until the end of the game, or they are eliminated (whichever happens first).
Each magical mechanism is given a number.
The first player then activates a roulette wheel in the centre of the play space (roll a die), whichever mechanism the roulette wheel lands on (value rolled) receives an extremely painful electric shock.
The player to the left is next to spin the wheel.
The mechanism is magically calibrated in order to determine exactly how much pain any individual can handle.
If a player receives two electrocutions they are knocked [[zzRules/DnD 5.5e Rules/04) Backlink Glossary/Conditions/Unconscious\|Unconscious]] for (1d8-Consitution modifier) minutes.
Alternatively a player may press a button on the side of the mechanism, allowing them to 'fold'. They are released from the mechanism, but forfeit any chance to win the pot.
When a player becomes [[zzRules/DnD 5.5e Rules/04) Backlink Glossary/Conditions/Unconscious\|Unconscious]], folds, or if they are the winner, their mechanism unlocks to release them.
The last conscious player receives the full pot.


### Life and Death
(1+ Players, with 1 dealer)
Life and death is a dice game played between the house (represented by a dealer) and a player. While up to five players can sit at a table, their only opponent is the house.

Any one table has a set ante, which could be 1cp, 5cp, 1sp, 1gp, or 10gp.

To play, each player places a bet; once bets have been placed, the house and the players each roll a d20, then:
1. A player rolls lower than the dealer, the house wins.
2. A player who rolls higher than the dealer reclaims the money they bet and wins that same amount from the house.
3. **War -*** A player who ties with the house has two options:
	- The player can surrender and lose half of their bet
	- The player can "go to war", in which case the player must double their bet, and then the player and the dealer both roll again.
4. ***Push your luck -*** When a player wins a roll of life and death, they can opt to push their luck on their next bet. In this case, they bet everything they won on the last roll (their ante plus their winnings from the house). If they win, the house pays double their bet.

### Sword and Shield
(3+ players)
Each player places an ante into the pot. The ante can be any amount, but must be matched by all players.
Each player then receives three tokens (which may be represented by individual dice).
The tokens are one each of a Sword, a Shield, and a Steal.

At the start of the round, each player secretly plays one of their tokens. Once all players have played their token, the results are revealed and the outcome determined;
1. Any player who plays a Sword token 'kills' the other players.
	- If more than one player plays a Sword token, they duel one another. The highest roll on a d20 'kills' all other players within the duel, no players outside of the duel are 'killed' this round.
2. Any player who plays a Shield token protects themselves. If a Sword token would have caused them to be 'killed', they are not.
3. Any player who plays the Steal token attempts to take the pot.
	- If a Sword token 'kills' a player who has attempted to Steal, the player does not take the pot.
4. Any player who is 'killed' is removed from the game.
5. The round concludes.
	- If any player is not 'killed', and played the Steal token, they have successfully stolen the pot and receive a share of the pot. The pot is split equally among all Steal players.
	- If only one player is not 'killed', that player takes the full pot.
	- If more than one player is not 'killed', and the pot has not been won then a new round begins with only the surviving players.
	- If all players in any given round choose to Steal the pot, then the pot is instead lost to the House.

### Three-Dragon Ante
(2+ Players)
Three dragon ante is played using an actual three-dragon ante card set - or otherwise can be played using d12s, and the following rules:

1. Randomly decide which participant is the dealer.
2. Each participant (including the dealer) places their opening bet, then rolls 5d12s to determine their hands, keeping these die rolls hidden from the other participants.
3. Starting to the left of the dealer and continuing counter clockwise, each participant reveals one of their dice rolls.
	a. This step is then repeated twice more.
		i. On the second and third round, each participant can raise their bet before revealing their next die roll.
		ii. The other participants then have three options: Match the bet, raise their bet (in which case all participants must match that bet in turn), or fold, forfeiring any bet they've placed and dropping out of the game.
4. Each participant who hasn't folded totals their die rolls. The one with the highest total wins. The winner becomes the dealer for the next game (assuming the participants want to keep playing).
