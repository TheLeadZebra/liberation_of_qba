> The journal is written mostly in common, however the lengthy, overly rhyming sentences don't really make much sense. On occasion there is the odd symbol or graphic. 
> Of note, a few pages into the book there is a surprisingly tasteful yet lewd drawing of a kneeling, nude purple dragonborn, with feminine features, large wings and a pointed tail. Their hands are bound together with a red ribbon whilst they look sheepishly off to the side.

Information that can be learned from the journal once translated from Thieves Cant- 
- Each Fairsday a section of the Drakenholm Barrier is deactivated in order to allow goods secretly to pass into or out of the city. Last week a 'Powerful Staff' was removed from the city, intended for The Desert region of the island.
- On the last Firesday of the last couple of months (26th), Alistair has attended a masquerade ball in The Amethyst Dragon's honour. The location changes, only known to invitees. Alistair believes it will always take place within one of the more well-to-do districts.
- Alistair sent out word that the smuggling through the Drakenholm Barrier should be put on hold due to Volker's death, but fears the reaction of the city nobility and The Amethyst Dragon if the Masquerade is understocked.
- There is talk that King's Landing will soon be shut off from incoming ships.
- The last page discusses Alistair's plans to insert The Amethyst Dragon gang into the island's history at [[Madam Twinblade's Waxwork Museum\|Madam Twinblade's Waxwork Museum]] in order for him to 'live on in memory'.
